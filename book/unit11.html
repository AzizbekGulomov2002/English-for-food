Macronutrients, are needed in large quantities by our bodies to provide energy and prevent disease. Macronutrients are the three basic nutrients that contain in our food: carbohydrates, proteins and fats. Carbohydrates give your body quick energy. They exist in foods such as bread, pasta and fruit. They come in three diverse forms monosaccharides, disaccharides and oligosaccharides. However, it is the glucose that propagates sugar that forms other saccharides and is the energy substrate for the regulation of red blood cells, white blood cells, renal medulla, brain and peripheral nerves. Protein gives our body important building blocks called amino acids that are needed for muscles, skin, blood, and the brain and nerves. Foods that are high in protein include animal foods such as meat and eggs. Protein is also found in beans, nuts, soybeans, legumes, and quinoa. Contrary to what most people think, your body needs fat to work well. It gives you energy, helps your body use vitamins, and keeps your organs safe. However, some fats are better than others.
