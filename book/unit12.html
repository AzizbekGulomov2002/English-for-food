Track 23. Listen to the passage in reading task.

Read the passage for overall understanding paying attention to the bolded words. Micronutrients are vitamins and minerals. The human body needs less micronutrients than macronutrients. The former mean “small nutrients” whereas the latter is “big nutrients”. The human body does not produce these essential substances for life, but absorbs them only through nutrition. Vitamins are organic compounds produced by plants and animals that can be broken down by heat, acid, or air. Minerals, on the other hand, are inorganic substances that exist in soil or water and cannot be broken down. When you eat, you consume vitamins made by plants and animals or minerals absorbed by them. Each food contains different micronutrients, so it's best to eat a variety of foods to ensure you are getting enough vitamins and minerals. micronutrients Vitamins and minerals are essential for growth, immune function, brain development, and many other important functions. The next element necessary for life is water. It is as essential as other nutrients for delivering oxygen throughout the body, preventing kidney disease, and other body functions. Most foods contain water, even when they appear solid and dry. The body can get about 20 percent of its total water needs from solid foods alone. A small amount of water is produced as a necessary product for the process of digesting food. Water obtained in this way provides about 10 percent of the body's water needs. The remaining 70% or more of water required by the body must come from the intake of various forms of fluids.

VI. Grammar. Comparing and contrasting. We can use these expressions in order to compare and contrast two things: X is as …as Y - Water is as essential as other nutrients. …whereas, - The micronutrients mean “small nutrients” whereas, macronutrients are “big nutrients”. …on the other hand… - Minerals, on the other hand, are inorganic substances that exist in soil or water and cannot be broken down.
