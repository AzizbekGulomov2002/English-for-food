III. Listening task 1. Track 27. Listen to the passage in reading task.

Processing grain into cereal involves several steps to transform raw grains like wheat, corn, oats, or rice into the familiar breakfast foods enjoyed by many. The process typically begins with cleaning the grains to remove impurities such as dirt, stones, and foreign matter. Once cleaned, the grains are often conditioned by soaking or steaming to soften the outer bran layer, making it easier to remove during milling. Milling is the next step, where the grains are ground into flour. This can be achieved using various methods, including stone milling or roller milling, which separates the bran and germ from the endosperm to produce different types of flour. The flour may undergo further processing, such as fortification with vitamins and minerals to enhance its nutritional value. It can also be mixed with water, yeast, and other ingredients to make dough for bread, or it can be cooked, extruded, and dried to produce breakfast cereals like flakes, puffs, or granola. Finally, the processed cereal products are packaged and distributed to consumers. From the farm to the breakfast table, the journey of grain into cereal involves careful processing and production to create nutritious and delicious foods enjoyed by people of all ages.

VI. Grammar. Food related PHRASAL VERBS 1. Eat in/out - To eat at home/to eat in a restaurant Are you going to eat in tonight? No, mum. I'm eating out with my friends.
2. Eat up - To finish all your meal, usually used with children Eat up your breakfast now or you'll be late for school.
3. Pick at - To eat small amounts of food because you're not hungry or you don't like it)
Is something wrong with you? You've been picking at your food for half an hour. 4. Dig in - To start eating Dig in before your food gets cold!5. Pig out (on) - To eat a lot of food all at once The buffet was great! I pigged out on cakes and biscuits.
6. Polish off - To eat something until it's finished Mansur polished off a plateful of lentils in next to no time.
7. Warm up - To heat food so that it's hot enough to eat Lunch is ready in the kitchen. When you get home you just have to warm it up.
8. Whip up - To prepare something to eat quickly I'll see what's in the fridge and whip up something for dinner.
9. Snack on - To eat small amounts of food between main meals or as a substitute for one of them Kamron is a bit hungry. He’ll snack on some cheese or biscuits.
10. Cut down (on) - To eat less of something, usually to improve your health The doctor told me to cut down on salt.
11. Bolt/Wolf down - Eat something quickly and almost without chewing I wolfed my breakfast down and went to work.
12. Live on - To eat a lot of a particular type of food Aziza could live on pasta. She loves it.
13. Thaw out - To take food out of the freezer so that we can cook it I think I'll thaw out some fish for dinner.
14. Go off - When food is not good to be eaten anymore Nadir forgot to freeze this meat and now it has gone off.
15. Order in - To call a restaurant so that they can prepare food for you and bring it to you
I don’t feel like cooking. Shall we order in some Chinese food?
