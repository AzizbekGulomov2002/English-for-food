Flour is a product obtained by or grinding grain. During the grinding process, the bran is separated from the grain and the endosperm is crushed to the required extent. Flour is divided into wheat flour, rye flour, barley flour, oat flour, corn flour, rice flour and other types, depending on the type of grain. Flour is a raw material for a number of sectors of the food industry, primarily for the bakery, confectionery and pasta industry. The main types of flour are wheat and rye flour. About 90% of the total produced flour corresponds to wheat flour. According to its properties, flour is divided into bread flour and pasta flour. The type of flour is determined by the amount obtained from 100 kg of grain, i.e. output. The higher the yield, the lower the quality of the flour. High, first and second grades of flour are obtained from wheat grain. Flour milling refers to a set of technological processes of processing grain into flour, which must satisfy the standard requirements of flour and have high consumption properties.
