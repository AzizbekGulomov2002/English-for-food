IX. Listening. Track 31. Listen to the audio and fill in the gaps.

Bread, a dietary staple for centuries, offers a range of essential nutrients vital for a balanced diet. Depending on the type of bread and its ingredients, nutritional values can vary significantly. Whole grain bread, rich in fiber, vitamins, and minerals, provides sustained energy and promotes digestive health. Its high fiber content supports bowel regularity and helps reduce the risk of chronic diseases like heart disease and type 2 diabetes. Additionally, whole grain breads contain B vitamins, essential for metabolism, and minerals such as iron and magnesium, crucial for overall health. White bread, while lower in fiber and nutrients compared to whole grain varieties, still serves as a valuable source of carbohydrates for energy. However, refined white flour lacks the bran and germ present in whole grains, resulting in fewer nutrients. Specialty breads like sourdough offer unique health benefits. The fermentation process involved in sourdough bread production enhances digestibility and nutrient absorption while providing probiotics that support gut health.
