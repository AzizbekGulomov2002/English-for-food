III. Listening task 1. Track 35. Listen to the passage in reading task.

Food waste management is a critical issue that affects both the environment and society. Every year, vast amounts of food are wasted, contributing to environmental degradation, resource depletion, and hunger. However, effective food waste management strategies can help mitigate these problems.One essential aspect of food waste management is prevention. This involves minimizing food waste at the source by adopting practices such as proper meal planning, portion control, and inventory management. By reducing over-purchasing and spoilage, individuals and businesses can significantly decrease the amount of food that ends up in landfills.Another key strategy is recycling and composting. Instead of disposing of food waste in landfills, where it emits harmful greenhouse gases, organic waste can be recycled or composted to produce nutrient-rich soil amendments. This not only diverts waste from landfills but also creates valuable resources for agriculture and gardening. Furthermore, food waste management encompasses donation initiatives aimed at redistributing surplus food to those in need. Food banks, shelters, and charitable organizations play a crucial role in collecting and distributing excess food to vulnerable populations, reducing food insecurity and alleviating hunger.

GLOBAL FOOD WASTE

Global food waste is a significant issue with far-reaching consequences for food waste management the environment, economy, and society. Every year, approximately one- third of all food produced for human consumption is wasted, amounting to over 1.3 billion tons of food annually. This wastage occurs at various stages of the food supply chain, from farm to fork. At the production stage, food is lost due to inefficiencies in harvesting, handling, and storage. Post-harvest losses are particularly prevalent in developing countries, where inadequate infrastructure and lack of access to technology contribute to significant food waste. In developed countries, consumer behavior and retail practices play a significant role in food waste. Supermarkets and consumers often discard edible food due to aesthetic imperfections, overstocking, or expiration dates, despite its safety and nutritional value. The environmental impact of global food waste is profound, contributing to greenhouse gas emissions, deforestation, and water scarcity. Moreover, wasted food represents a squandered opportunity to address global hunger and malnutrition.
