IV. Reading task. Read the passage for overall understanding paying attention to the bolded words.
Alcoholic drinks are drinks containing ethyl alcohol (alcohol). Alcoholic beverages include alcohol, vodka, liqueur-vodka products, rum, whiskey, and wines. Food ethyl alcohol is made from raw materials containing starch or sugar. Rectified ethyl alcohol (C2H5OH) is a colorless, clear liquid without foreign tastes and odors. Vodka is obtained by mixing rectified alcohol with softened water. A small amount of invert sugar, soda and citric acid, sugar and skimmed milk, etc. are added to vodka in order to soften and improve its taste. Liqueurs are characterized by high aromatic properties and high sugar content. Depending on the content of alcohol and the raw materials used, liqueurs are divided into strong, dessert and cream types. Rum is a strong alcoholic drink made from alcohol obtained by fermenting sugarcane sugar and cane molasses. Rum is a light brown clear solution with a unique smell, slightly burning taste, and contains 45% alcohol and 2% sugar.
Whiskey is a strong alcoholic drink with 45% alcohol content. Whiskey made from grains (barley, corn or their mixture) is made by keeping alcohol in charred barrels for 3-10 years. Grape wine is made by ripening grapes. The alcohol content of grape wines can be from 9% to 20%. depending on the color, grape wines are divided into white, pink and red wines. Depending on the technology of preparation, wines are divided into light, fortified, flavored, playful, sparkling or carbonated types.
Cognac is a high-strength alcoholic drink (it contains 40-57% alcohol), which is made from brandy alcohol by distilling still wines. Cognac's golden color and complex aroma are formed as a result of keeping cognac in oak barrels for several years (not less than 3 years).
