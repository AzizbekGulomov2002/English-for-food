Chocolate is a confectionery product obtained by mixing the products obtained from the processing of cocoa beans (crushed cocoa and cocoa butter) with sugar and is characterized by a dark brown, shiny surface, pleasant taste and elegant aroma. Depending on the recipe and the method of processing, chocolate is divided into simple, dessert, porous and spicy chocolates. Nut, fruit and other various confectionery textures are used as technology of chocolate progucts ingredients in chocolate preparation. As a result, the particles of such chocolate mass are very small, and the chocolate mass itself is elegant. Plain, dessert and porous chocolates are prepared with or without adding various tasty and aromatic substances. Dry milk, dry cream, roasted nut kernels, coffee, waffles, cookies and the like are used as such additives in the preparation of chocolate. Additive-free (original) chocolate is made from crushed cocoa, cocoa butter and powdered sugar. The resulting product is called original chocolate. Chocolate is stored in dry, clean, ventilated rooms at a temperature of 18-3 degrees, with relative air humidity not exceeding 75%. If these conditions are observed, the shelf life of chocolate without additives is 6 months, and that of chocolate with additives and ingredients is 3 months. V. Reading comprehension.
