IV. Reading task. Read the passage for overall understanding paying attention to the bolded words. Fats are a complex mixture of organic compounds with very similar physico-chemical properties in the tissues of plants and living organisms. They are distinguished from other substances by the following properties: - insolubility in water (hydrophobicity) and solubility in organic solvents (gasoline, chloroform, hexane, etc.); - the presence of high molecular fatty acids, carbohydrate radicals and complex ether groups corresponding to them. Such substances can have a great physiological effect on body description of fats tissues. Fats are one of the most important components of food products. They are used in the production of various consumer products, and this is one of the factors that determine the nutritional and biological value of consumer products. In terms of energy value (caloric content), fats make up 30-35% of a person's need for food products, that is, an average of 100-108 g per day, including direct fat-oil products as 50-52 g should be consumed. Long-term non-consumption of fats, low consumption or consumption of fats that contain few necessary biologically active components leads to negative physiological changes in the body: central nervous system dysfunction, decreased immunity and life may be shortened.
