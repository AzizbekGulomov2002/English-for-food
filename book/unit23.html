IV. Reading task. Read the passage for overall understanding paying attention to the bolded words. The process of cleaning oil from unwanted lipid groups and impurities is called refining. The purpose of refining is to cleanly separate triacylglycerols from other lipids and impurities from natural oils and fats. But in all cases, refining is not carried out until all structural lipids and impurities are lost. It depends on the purpose of the oil. But any oil must first be completely cleaned of mechanical impurities and water. This condition is fulfilled at the stage of primary purification of oil in oil-oil production enterprises. Strainers, centrifuges, filter presses and other devices are used to separate solid compounds and water from oil. Due to the fact that lipids, which are part of natural oils and fats, have different physical and chemical properties, modern refining is a complex process consisting of a sequence of technological processes that differ in the nature of physical and chemical effects on these lipids. The most basic condition for applying any technological process in refining is to preserve triacylglycerols in oils and fats in their native, i.e. natural form. The modern technology of full refining envisages cleaning the oil from phospholipids, wax and waxy substances, free fatty acids, coloring substances. These processes are called hydration, crystallization, neutralization, oil whitening, oil staining (deodorization) processes. Full refinement is not always necessary. Salad oils used directly for food, margarine, confectionery and culinary products, and oils and fats used in the production of mayonnaise are fully refined. In other cases, for example, in the production of saturated fats, extraction and deodorization processes may not be performed. At present, distillation refining without alkaline neutralization is recommended for fats and oils with a low content of free fatty acids. At the same time, free fatty acids and volatile substances that give taste and smell are separated from the oil.
