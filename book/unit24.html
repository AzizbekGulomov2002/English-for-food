IV. Reading task. Read the passage for overall understanding paying attention to the bolded words.
nutritional value of meat One of the main branches of the food industry in our republic is the meat industry. It is known that meat and meat products are necessary and important for every human body because they contain a large amount of valuable proteins. Meat proteins are several times higher than vegetable proteins, so they are an important source of energy. Considering the main composition of the protein product, it consists of a combination of different amino acids. Proteins are divided into complete and incomplete proteins depending on the amount of amino acids. It is known that an adult needs 105-150 g of protein per day. It has been determined that quality meat contains an average of 20% protein. The energy value (caloric content) of meat depends on its quality, fatness and leanness of the animal, feeding method, nutrition, age, gender, physiological state, etc., and it was tested to be approximately 1200-2800 kilocalories per kilogram. Beef contains 72-75% water and 25-28% dry matter. But almost 60% of the dry residue is protein, 5% is fat, and 1-1.2% are minerals, vitamins, enzymes and hormones. 85% of the meat protein consists of amino acids that are full value and irreplaceable. Nucleoproteins, which are considered extremely complex, are also found in meat. These are ribonucleic and deoxyribonucleic acids, elastin, collagen mucoproteins. Minerals (potassium, sodium, calcium, magnesium, iron, even phosphorus and copper) occupy a significant place in beef. It is known that the increase of fatty substances in meat leads to the decrease of mineral substances in it.

VIII. Classroom activity. “Role playing” method. nutritional value of meat Ask two students to act as nutririonist and customer. Give some help for language and vocabulary. Encourage them to make their dialogue as informative and interesting as possible. Example:
Nutritionist: Hello! How are you today? Client: I'm doing well, thank you. I've been thinking a lot about my diet lately, and I wanted to get your opinion on something. Nutritionist: Of course, I'm here to help. What's on your mind? Client: Well, I've been considering reducing my meat consumption. I've heard a lot about the health and environmental benefits of eating less meat, but I'm not sure how to go about it. Nutritionist: That's a great topic to discuss. Reducing meat intake can indeed have numerous health benefits, such as lowering the risk of heart disease and certain cancers. It can also have positive environmental impacts by reducing greenhouse gas emissions and conserving water and land resources. Client: That's exactly what I've read. But I'm concerned about getting enough protein in my diet if I cut back on meat. How can I ensure I'm still meeting my nutritional needs? Nutritionist: That's a valid concern, but there are plenty of plant-based sources of protein that you can incorporate into your diet. Foods like beans, lentils, tofu, tempeh, nuts, seeds, and whole grains are all excellent sources of protein. By including a variety of these foods in your meals, you can easily meet your protein requirements. Client: That sounds manageable. I also enjoy the taste of meat, so I'm worried about missing out on that flavor. Nutritionist: There are many plant-based meat alternatives available nowadays that mimic the taste and texture of meat quite well. These products can be used in place of meat in your favorite recipes, providing you with a similar flavor experience without the negative health and environmental impacts associated with conventional meat consumption. Client: That's reassuring to hear. I think I'm ready to start making some changes to my diet. Thank you so much for your guidance! Nutritionist: You're very welcome! I'm glad I could help. Remember, making small, gradual changes to your diet is often the most sustainable approach. If you have any more questions or need further assistance, don't hesitate to reach out.
