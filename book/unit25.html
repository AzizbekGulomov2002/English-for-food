Track 49. Listen to the passage in reading task.

Milk is a very valuable food product. It is a dispersed medium (a solution of plasma-mineral salts and milk sugar). It consists of a colloidal phase (proteins and salts) and a small dispersed phase (milk fat). Milk contains an average of 3.8% milk fat, 4.7% milk sugar, 3.3% protein, 0.7% mineral substances and 87.5% water. The milk and milk products industry has the main sectors such as the production of milk and cheese products, butter and milk preserves. Dairy products production network supplies the population with various pasteurized and sterilized milk and cream, fermented milk products, butter, cheese, ice cream. Currently, in many countries, new technological processes, pasteurization of dairy products using infrared rays and high- frequency electricity have been launched. The acidity of milk depends on its salt, protein, carbohydrates, citric acid and others. Lactic acid accumulates in milk as a result of fermentation of milk sugar under the influence of milk bacteria. Freshness of milk is judged by its acidity. Milk density is one of the main indicators of milk quality. It shows how much milk mass is greater than water mass in the same volume. Milk density depends on the amount of dry substances (fat, sugar, protein) in milk. The boiling temperature of milk is slightly higher than that of water - equal to 100.2 C.
