Track 55. Listen to the passage in reading task.

Nutritional acids, dyes, and flavorings play significant roles in the food industry, affecting taste, appearance, and even shelf life. These additives are commonly used in processed foods, beverages, and snacks to enhance their sensory appeal and nutritional profile. Nutritional acids, such as citric acid and acetic acid, are natural compounds found in fruits, vegetables, and dairy products. They are often used as preservatives to inhibit bacterial growth, regulate acidity levels, and add tanginess to foods. Citric acid, for instance, not only imparts a sour taste but also helps preserve the vibrant color of fruits in canned form. Dyes are synthetic or natural substances added to food to alter its color, making it more visually appealing. Synthetic dyes like FD&C Red No. 40 and Yellow No. 5 are widely used in candies, beverages, and processed snacks. Natural dyes, derived from sources like beets and turmeric, are gaining popularity as consumers seek cleaner label options. Flavorings, both natural and artificial, are added to food to enhance or mimic specific tastes and aromas. These can range from simple vanilla and almond extracts to complex formulations designed to replicate the flavors of fruits, spices, or savory ingredients.V. Reading comprehension.
