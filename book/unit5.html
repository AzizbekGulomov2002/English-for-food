IV. Reading task.

Read the passage for overall understanding.
According to scientific data, there are approximately 100 trillion cells in the human body, and 90 trillion of them, that is, 9 out of 10, are bacteria of various forms. These tiny organisms are bacteria, viruses, yeasts and protozoa.
Bacteria are unicellular organisms that contain the most important components for survival and reproduction - chromosome, ribosome, cytoplasm and outer membrane. Bacteria are one of the most important microorganisms for food processing. Most of them are harmless and even useful, and some indicate the presence of disease in the body. Although there are thousands of species of bacteria, they are all single-celled and come in three basic shapes: spherical, straight rods, and spiral rods. Some common foodborne bacteria mainly affect children, the bacteria elderly and immunocompromised people.
Lactobacillus species are an important component of human and animal microbiota and improve the functioning of a number of our organs, including the digestive system. They are one of the most common probiotics found in food products such as yogurt. The spherical bacterium staphylococcus is found mainly in water, in air droplets, in the normal microflora of humans and animals and causes disease. Poisoning (toxic infection) is observed when eating milk and dairy products, cake, ice cream and other food products containing pathogenic staphylococcus.

VI. Grammar. Quantifiers. A few / Few, A little / Little
Few = not many Little = not much
Few normally conveys a negative idea.
- He has few friends.		 Little normally conveys a negative idea.
- There was little time to finish it.
A few = some (a small amount) A little = some (a small amount)
A few normally conveys a positive idea.
- He has a few friends that will help.		 A little normally conveys a positive idea.
- I have a little milk you can use.

VII. Classroom activity.Story writers! METHOD

Ask students to come to take cards turned back and sit according to “the little, a little, few, a few” cards. Then write several sentences with gaps to fill out with quantifiers. Students are asked to attach the appropriate quantifier in the gaps. At the end of the activity motivate them to make their own sentences for the quantifier they have taken
