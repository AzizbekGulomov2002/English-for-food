IV. Reading task. Read the passage for overall understanding.

Chemistry in food science is the study of properties, composition and chemical change which take place in processing and storage. It is interrelated with other subjects like chemistry, molecular biology, botany, zoology and other biology chemical sciences. Food chemists deal with biological matters of dead or dying that are post harvest physiology of plants and changes in different environmental conditions. That is why a food chemistry is responsible for conditions suitable for sustaining the residual life processes (post harvest physiology) for example fresh fruits and vegetables during their marketing. Ensuring nutritious and safe food products is also one of the concerns of the science and thanks to it consumers can be aware of information on food food chemistry they are purchasing. Nutrition studies reveal that the human body needs certain nutrients like carbohydrates, proteins and fats in large quantities and some others like vitamins and minerals in much smaller quantities. Today, sensory qualities or external qualities of food including color, appearance, firmness, mouth feel, flavor, taste can be controlled through chemical reaction. Changes in these qualities, for example in fruits affects the value reducing in the market. For example, loss of the green color of spinach on storage or cooking is due to degradation of chlorophyll. Similarly, browning of cut apples is initiated due to enzymatic oxidation of phenolic substances. Softening of fruits for example is due to the breakdown of pectins or toughening of meat is due to post mortem chemical changes resulting in pH reduction and tissue hardening. Changes in flavor is also because of chemical reactions. For example, flavor change in fatty foods called rancidity is due to oxidation of unsaturated fatty acids. Consumers know about these in subsequent units. What is important is that once you know the chemical cause of quality deterioration, it is possible to devise methods to control it.
